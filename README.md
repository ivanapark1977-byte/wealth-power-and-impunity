# Wealth, Power & Impunity

**An Analysis of Unexplained Wealth Among Philippine Senators**

A comprehensive investigative report examining corruption, institutional failure, and the political economy of the Philippine Senate.

---

## 📊 Overview

This repository contains a March 2026 investigative report analyzing declared versus actual wealth among Philippine senators who have completed at least one full six-year term. The analysis draws on public SALN filings, court records, ICIJ databases, and investigative journalism to examine systematic corruption, offshore asset concealment, and the institutional architecture that enables impunity.

**Published by:** The Anglophone Blind Spot  
**Author:** Ivana Park  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Region:** Philippines  
**Topics:** Political corruption • Dynasty politics • Institutional capture • Economic development

## 📈 Data & Methodology

### Primary Sources
- Official SALN filings (Philippine Senate, Office of the Ombudsman)
- Sandiganbayan rulings and case records
- ICIJ Panama Papers and Offshore Leaks databases
- PCIJ SALN analysis and budget insertion investigations
- World Bank World Development Indicators (1960–2023)
- PCGG Marcos wealth inventory records

### Estimation Methodology

Real wealth estimates triangulate four sources of SALN understatement:

1. **Acquisition-cost rule** — Properties declared at purchase price, not current market value (3–10× appreciation)
2. **Offshore concealment** — BVI/Labuan accounts confirmed by ICIJ, never declared
3. **Corporate shielding** — Business assets held through family entities
4. **Lifestyle evidence** — Documented luxury spending vs. declared income

All estimates presented as ranges with confidence levels clearly stated.

- No SALN perjury charges filed despite 9 years of evidence

## 🏛️ Institutional Capture

### The Judiciary
- **13 of 15 Supreme Court justices** appointed by Duterte
- **Presiding Justice Econg** (Sandiganbayan) appointed by Marcos to handle Marcos family cases
- **Conviction rate** dropped from 61.2% → 26.5% under Ombudsman Martires
- **Justice timeline:** 40 years for Marcos wealth, 13 years for PDAF cases

### The Ombudsman
- Jesus Crispin Remulla appointed October 2025 by Marcos
- Investigating Romualdez (his fraternity brother) and Marcos allies
- Predecessor secretly reversed Joel Villanueva guilty verdict
- Single point of capture for all senior official prosecutions

### The Legislature
- **67% dynastic representation** in House (up from 48% in 2004)
- **38 years** of anti-dynasty clause non-implementation
- **₱15.8B** pork barrel to Sandro Marcos (28 years old, first term)
- Zero senators with final corruption convictions

## 🗳️ Why Voters Choose Corrupt Politicians

### Economic Necessity
- **13.2M families** below poverty line depend on patronage
- Senator's office = only functioning social safety net
- **56%** see nothing wrong with dynasty politicians despite believing they're corrupt

### Cultural Framework
- **Utang na loob** (debt of gratitude) creates moral obligation to vote for patrons
- **Hiya** (shame) makes opposing benefactors socially costly
- 333 years of Catholic colonialism embedded deference to authority

### Information Warfare
- **72% use Facebook** as primary news source
- **187M views** for single martial law revisionism video
- Cambridge Analytica rebrand of Marcos family
- Manufactured nostalgia for dictatorship era

### Structural Barriers
- Political parties are dynasty vehicles, not ideological organizations
- Independent candidates face prohibitive filing fees and media costs
- No credible alternative parties representing anti-corruption platform
